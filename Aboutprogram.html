REWARD (World Bank funded)

Rejuvenating Watersheds for Agricultural Resilience through Innovative Development

  • Karnataka Watershed Development Department implemented Sujala-III from 2013 to 2019 with the World Bank assistance. First time in the country, Land Resource Inventory (LRI) was generated for 2534 MWs covering 14 lakh ha rainfed area of 12 districts of the State, using innovative scientific tools through consortium of scientific partner institutes.

  • Sujala-III has created way forward for formulating watershed development programs with application of advanced scientific data and tools and move towards more sustainable and resilient agriculture.

  • In view of Sujala-III experience, Karnataka State is implementing in World Bank funded Multi-State Watershed Development program and identified as a Light house Partner to provide technical guidance for other States.

  • GoK has approved to implement REWARD in 25 districts with a budget of Rs.600.00 Crores (World Bank loan 70% & State share 30%) and implementation commenced from 2022-23.

  • 20 Project Districts: Chamarajanagar, Davanagere, Tumkur, Koppal, Kalaburagi, Yadagiri, Gadag, Bidar, Vijayapura, Chikkamagaluru, Raichur, Haveri, Dharwad, Belagavi, Bagalkot, Shivamogga, Chitradurga, Chikkaballapura, Kolar and Hassan

  • REWARD is a result based “PforR” (Program for Results) financing mode.

  • Disbursement of funds from World Bank is based on achievement of identified target in the form of Disbursement Linked Indicators (DLIs). The DLIs will verified by an Independent Verification Agency (IVA), NABCONS (NABARD Consultancy Services) selected as IVA for Karnataka.

Project Development Objective:

  • To strengthen capacities of partner institutions and stakeholders to adopt improved watershed management.

  • Increasing Agriculture resilience of the farmers by adopting advanced scientific data and tools.

  • Support value chains development in selected watersheds.

  • Implementation of REWARD program is aimed at collection of Land Resource information (LRI) scientifically to issue Land Resource (LRI) cards comprising site- specific recommendations for farmers to use at field level, and for preparation and implementation of scientific Detailed project reports (DPR) for watershed development projects.

Major components of REWARD:

  • Land resource inventory (LRI) in 25 lakh ha rainfed watershed area

  • Watershed development on Saturation mode in 1 lakh ha (20 SWs) based on LRI & Hydrology recommendations

  • FPO and Value chain development: 28 FPOs

  • Providing improved Agro-met advisory services to farmers

  • Establishing Centre of Excellence on Science based Watershed Management at UAS Bengaluru

Other Activities

  • Innovative Pilot studies for input management using LRI data

  • Improving O & M policy for maintenance of assets created in the project

  • Up-gradation of Digital Library, DSS & LRI Portal

Major Technical Partners: National Bureau of Soil Survey & Land Use Planning (ICAR-NBSS&LUP), Indian Institute of Science (IISc), all 5 State Agricultural & Horticultural Universities, Karnataka State Remote Sensing and Application Centre (KSRSAC) Karnataka State Natural Disaster Monitoring Centre (KSNDMC) and Visvesvaraya Technological University (VTU), Belagavi.
NBSS&LUP is the lead partner for LRI and IISc is for Hydrological studies.

REWARD Salient Features:

  1. Generation of cadastral level (Survey No. wise) Land resource information using RS, GIS and other advanced scientific tools and technologies.

  2. Development of criteria and guidelines for interpretation and generation of Thematic maps and Site-specific recommendations using LRI data.

  3. Understanding hydrological dynamics, hydro-geology and climatic variability and develop tools to measure the Ground water levels, Runoff and soil moisture.

  4. Digitization & Integration of all the data in a single platform for planning & providing site specific informations to develop “One area One plan” – to save resources, funds and time constraints.

  5. Developing Protocol for demystifying the science to community through consultation process and thus reducing watershed development cycle.

  6. Evidence based Monitoring and Impact evaluation of the project interventions.

  7. Consortium approach in achieving objectives - Scientific research institutes associate as project stakeholders.

  8. Establishing CoE plays a critical role in building capacity of all the States on LRI and operationalisation of future generation PMKSY-WDC programs in the country.

Outcome of REWARD is expected to consolidate & improve the existing guidelines and formulate appropriate protocols & new set of guidelines for science-based watershed planning, convergence of schemes and other interventions in the country later.
